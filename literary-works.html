Literary Works

The writings that ignited Philippine nationalism

Major Literary Contributions

Rizal's written works that exposed colonial injustices and inspired revolution

Major Novels

Noli Me Tángere (1887)

Rizal's first novel exposing the abuses of Spanish friars and colonial government in the Philippines.

  • Genre: Political novel
  • Language: Spanish
  • Published: Berlin, Germany

El Filibusterismo (1891)

The sequel to Noli Me Tángere, exploring themes of revolution and reform.

  • Genre: Political novel
  • Language: Spanish
  • Published: Ghent, Belgium

Notable Poems

Mi Último Adiós (1896)

Rizal's final poem written on the eve of his execution, expressing his love for the Philippines.

A la Juventud Filipina (1879)

Prize-winning poem encouraging Filipino youth to use their talents for their country.

El Canto del Viajero

Poem reflecting on his travels and experiences in foreign lands.

Essays and Articles

La Indolencia de los Filipinos

Essay defending Filipinos against accusations of laziness, attributing it to colonial conditions.

Filipinas Dentro de Cien Años

Essay predicting the future of the Philippines within a hundred years.

Contributions to La Solidaridad

Numerous articles published under pseudonyms in the reformist newspaper.
