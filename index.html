The Man Behind the Legend

Understanding the life and contributions of the Philippines' most revered national hero

José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda (June 19, 1861 – December 30, 1896) was a Filipino nationalist, writer, and polymath whose works and ideals sparked the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule.

As a visionary reformist, Rizal advocated for peaceful change through education and civic reform. His novels, Noli Me Tángere and El Filibusterismo, exposed the injustices of Spanish colonial rule and awakened Filipino national consciousness.

A true Renaissance man, Rizal was not only a novelist but also a physician, sculptor, painter, linguist, scientist, and farmer. His multifaceted talents and unwavering patriotism made him a symbol of Filipino intellect and dignity.

Quick Facts

Born: June 19, 1861, Calamba, Laguna
Died: December 30, 1896, Manila (executed)
Languages: 22 including Spanish, French, German
Education: University of Santo Tomas, Madrid, Heidelberg

Literary Legacy

His novels are considered foundational texts of Philippine literature and nationalism.

Medical Practice

As an ophthalmologist, he restored sight to his mother and many countrymen.

Global Citizen

Traveled extensively through Europe, Asia, and America, absorbing diverse cultures.

Peaceful Reformer

Advocated for change through education and civic participation rather than violence.
