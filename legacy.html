Legacy

The enduring impact of José Rizal on Philippine history and identity

Enduring Impact

How Rizal's life and works continue to influence the Philippines and the world

As National Hero

Official Recognition

Declared the national hero of the Philippines by the American colonial government and later affirmed by the Philippine government.

Rizal Day

December 30, the anniversary of his execution, is celebrated as Rizal Day, a national holiday in the Philippines.

Rizal Monument

The Rizal Monument in Luneta Park, Manila, serves as a symbol of Filipino gratitude and a tomb containing his remains.

Educational Legacy

Rizal Law (Republic Act 1425)

Requires all educational institutions in the Philippines to offer courses about José Rizal, particularly his novels Noli Me Tángere and El Filibusterismo.

Rizal's Works in Curriculum

His novels are required reading in Philippine schools and universities, ensuring each generation learns about his ideals.

Rizal's Educational Philosophy

Advocated for education as the means to liberation, influencing Philippine educational philosophy.

Cultural Influence

In Arts and Media

Numerous films, plays, novels, and artworks have been created about Rizal's life and works.

Rizal as Symbol

His image appears on Philippine currency, postage stamps, and various national symbols.

International Recognition

Recognized worldwide as an intellectual and reformist, with monuments and streets named after him in various countries.

Philosophical Contributions

"The youth is the hope of our future."

Rizal's belief in the power of education and the role of youth continues to inspire generations of Filipinos to pursue excellence and serve their country.
