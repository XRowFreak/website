Life & Timeline

The chronological journey of José Rizal's remarkable life

Chronological Life Events

A detailed timeline of Rizal's life from birth to martyrdom

1861

Birth in Calamba

José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda was born on June 19, 1861, in Calamba, Laguna. He was the seventh child of Francisco Mercado and Teodora Alonso.

1872

Execution of Gomburza

The execution of three Filipino priests (Mariano Gómez, José Burgos, and Jacinto Zamora) deeply affected the young Rizal and inspired his nationalist sentiments.

1882

Travel to Europe

Rizal traveled to Spain to continue his studies. He enrolled at the Universidad Central de Madrid where he studied medicine, philosophy, and letters.

1887

Publication of Noli Me Tángere

Rizal published his first novel, Noli Me Tángere, in Berlin. The novel exposed the injustices of Spanish colonial rule in the Philippines.

1891

Publication of El Filibusterismo

His second novel, El Filibusterismo, was published in Ghent. This sequel continued to criticize Spanish colonial rule and explored themes of revolution.

1892

Exile to Dapitan

Rizal was exiled to Dapitan in Mindanao where he spent four years. During this time, he practiced medicine, taught school, and engaged in community projects.

1896

Execution at Bagumbayan

On December 30, 1896, Rizal was executed by firing squad at Bagumbayan (now Luneta Park). His death sparked the Philippine Revolution against Spanish rule.
